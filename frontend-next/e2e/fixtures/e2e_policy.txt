Acme Finance Limited internal Know Your Customer and Anti Money Laundering Policy

Customer identification is completed at onboarding using an Officially Valid Document such as a passport, driving licence, or Aadhaar number.
Customer due diligence is performed for every new relationship before an account is opened.
Records of identification data and transactions are retained for five years after the business relationship ends.
The Principal Officer is responsible for reporting suspicious transactions to FIU-IND without undue delay.
Customers are classified into low, medium, and high risk categories based on their profile and product usage.
Enhanced due diligence applies to high-risk customers, including senior management approval before onboarding.
Periodic KYC updation is carried out every two years for high-risk customers and every ten years for low-risk customers.
Video-based Customer Identification Process may be used for remote onboarding where permitted by the regulator.
The beneficial owner of a legal entity customer is identified and verified before establishing the relationship.
Screening against applicable sanctions lists is performed at onboarding and on an ongoing basis.
Staff receive annual training on Know Your Customer and anti money laundering obligations.
Internal audit reviews the effectiveness of these controls once a year and reports to the board.
